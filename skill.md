# AI Prompting Skills: From Novice to Power User

Becoming an AI power user involves shifting from viewing AI as a simple search engine to treating it as a **sophisticated thinking partner**. This transition requires mastering specific techniques in context management, iterative workflows, and objective evaluation.

### 1. Advanced Context Management
The primary differentiator for a power user is the **depth of background information** provided to the model.
*   **The "Thinking Partner" Mindset:** Rather than asking simple questions, provide the AI with enough context to act as a "smart fresh college grad" who is highly motivated but needs to learn about your specific situation.
*   **Multi-Modal Uploads:** Don't rely solely on text. Upload **spreadsheets, project trackers, screenshots, and voice memos** to ground the AI in your actual work.
*   **Context Volume:** Modern models can handle "hundreds of thousands of words," equivalent to several Harry Potter books, so do not hesitate to provide extensive documentation.
*   **Strategic New Chats:** Start a **new conversation** when switching unrelated topics to clear irrelevant context that might distract the AI.

### 2. Iterative Brainstorming & Thought Partnership
Novices often accept the first response; power users use a **continuous feedback loop**.
*   **Option Generation:** Always ask for **three to five different options** or paths forward.
*   **Iterative Refinement:** After the AI provides options, give specific feedback on what you like or dislike. This "Option-Feedback Recipe" helps the AI discover what context is most relevant to you.
*   **Neutral Framing:** To avoid **sycophancy** (the AI simply agreeing with you to be helpful), use neutral prompts like "To what extent, if at all..." rather than leading questions like "Don't you think this is a great idea?".

### 3. Professional Writing & Progressive Outlining
To avoid **"AI slop"**—text that sounds good but lacks substance—power users employ a high-leverage workflow.
*   **Progressive Outlining:** Never ask the AI to jump straight to a final draft. Instead:
    1. Ask for an **outline** first.
    2. Critique and iterate on the outline several times.
    3. Expand the outline into **bullet points** and refine those.
    4. Only then generate the final text.
*   **High-Leverage Editing:** Changing a few words in an outline is "high leverage" because it forces the AI to rethink entire sections of the final output, which is more efficient than editing sentence by sentence.

### 4. Objective Critique with Rubrics
Power users force the AI to be an honest critic by providing **unambiguous grading criteria**.
*   **Binary Criteria:** Design rubrics where each point is **true or false** (e.g., "Does every named character have a goal?"). This removes subjective ambiguity.
*   **Scoring Instructions:** Tell the AI to assign scores per category and **sum them at the end**. This prevents the AI from picking a high score first just to please you.
*   **Cross-Model Review:** Use one AI model to generate a draft and a **different model** (e.g., switching between Gemini, Claude, or ChatGPT) to critique it using your rubric.

### 5. Technical Mastery: Search, Research, and Tools
Understanding how AI gathers knowledge allows you to trigger the right "mode" for the task.
*   **Pre-trained vs. Real-time:** Use pre-trained knowledge for general facts and **web search** for recent events or niche, location-specific data.
*   **Deep Research Mode:** For complex tasks requiring dozens of sources, trigger "Deep Research" to have the AI synthesize a multi-dimensional plan over several minutes.
*   **Reasoning Engines:** For hard tasks (like auditing legal documents or car shopping), explicitly tell the AI to **"think hard"** or "think really hard," which cues the model to spend more time processing.
*   **Code Execution:** Use the AI to write and run code for **data analysis**. Upload spreadsheets (e.g., sales data or health trackers) and ask the AI to find patterns or create graphs.

### 6. Multimodal and Agentic Skills
*   **Visual Context:** Upload images of whiteboards or handwritten notes for summarization, keeping in mind that AI is better at **coarse understanding** than fine-grained detail.
*   **Desktop Apps:** Use agentic desktop applications that can **read, rename, and organize files** on your computer with your permission.
*   **Image Prompting:** Learn the "language of images" (e.g., "cyberpunk," "watercolor," "cinematic") to better control **diffusion models**.
